Mayan: Science and Technology

Introduction

This Belizean History Course has taught me a lot about our past, and our role we play in the grander scheme of things. The topics I've found most interesting and significant is of the ancient civilization of the Mayas. Like many other countries, such as Egypt, India, Greece and so on, they too have advanced ancient civilizations who have done astonishing things that still left them bewildered and in awe as to how they've managed to bring together societies governed by intricate sciences. In the case of the Mayan, we've made many discoveries and uncovered many artifacts and manuscripts that are a true testament to the Mayan's knowledge in science. The Mayans were also known for their agricultural practices such as slash and burn and other things like terrace systems and drainage canals. Other things their known for is their mathematics system and calendar and their advanced understanding in astronomy. Many of the practices and knowledge from ancient civilizations are still used to this day, which really shows the significance of their pioneering through history. Because of their efforts and their unfortunate downfalls throughout history we are able to learn and utilize their works. For example we can keep track of the time by looking at our watches or our phones, the Mayans kept track of time and many significant seasons and times of the year by tracking the sun and the planets. Another reason why we should be grateful and acknowledge the Mayans and many other ancient civilizations because they've paved the road for us to continue develop.
